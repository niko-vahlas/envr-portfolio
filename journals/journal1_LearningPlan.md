I took ENVR 330 as I wanted to become a better researcher, and I was also looking for an excuse to explore a facet of science that I had not had the chance to engage with: environmental science. Climate change is something that worries me, and while I understand this is not a climate change focused course, I’m hopeful that I will still gain some meaningful exposure at a university level.

My goal of becoming a better researcher is quite broad, so I came up with a list of skills that I believe make a good researcher and then picked skills from that list to focus on improving for the term. I will improve my skills on finding credible sources, synthesizing information, and communicating results. I will do this by seeking out feedback as frequently as possible from peers, teaching assistants, and of course, the professor. For my goal of learning about climate change, if I have an opportunity to select my own research topic, I will select something climate change related.

I think ENVR 330 will be impactful beyond this class, and even beyond an academic setting. Skills like synthesizing information and clearly communicating results are important, and they will serve me well in my career as a software developer. I recently had an internship, and the bulk of my time was not writing code, but writing documents for others to read, much like research.
