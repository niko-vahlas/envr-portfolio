In just two weeks of class, my approach to reading scientific papers has significantly changed, as I now consider the scientific process while reading the paper. Interestingly enough, the assignment where we had to read the “Communication of the role of natural variability in future North American climate” is what led to this. The rubric for that assignment said that we must identify the motivation, research questions, methods, conclusions, validation, and implications.
Before this assignment, I tended to read scientific papers as though I was reading a novel, line by line, and not thinking too carefully about what I was reading. But when I looked at the rubric, I realized that attributes such as motivation, research question(s), etc., are a part of the scientific process, are in every scientific paper, and thus I should be actively searching for those things and keeping in mind what they are while reading the paper. I read “Communication of the role of natural variability in future North American climate” in this manner, and I noticed I was able to comprehend what was occurring in the paper much more easily, as I had mental notes corresponding to each of those earlier-mentioned attributes.
This has changed how I read scientific papers, and from now on, I will be carefully considering the scientific process while reading. This will impact me much further than ENVR 300, as this will help me at any time I need to read an academic paper throughout my life.
