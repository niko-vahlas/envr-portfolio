My understanding of environmental models has changed significantly. The moment that everything changed was when my group discussed the limitations of our model, and I realized that, despite all of the hard work we had put in, in the real world, our model would not be nearly good enough to be put to use. I realized that, in reality, there are countless converters and countless processes affecting each of our phosphorus reservoirs.

This moment was extremely significant for me, as previously I had incorrectly assumed that many scientific models were quite simple, and I questioned why certain models could not be more accurate. I had read about models that had made predictions decades in advance, but ended up being incorrect. I was overly critical in these situations, as I figured that creating an accurate model shouldn't be that difficult.

This moment certainly will have a large and pronounced effect on my future. First, in the workplace, I will be very cautious about promises I make to superiors when I model any sort of outcome that may have many different variables affecting it. Even if I think that it sounds simple, I will do my due diligence first, and then be up front regarding limitations. This will help me perform better in my future career as a software engineer. Secondly, I will be far less critical when I hear about models that made predictions that turned out to be incorrect.
